Introduction to Phishing Threats

Phishing attacks are one of the most common security threats facing organisations today. They attempt to trick you into revealing sensitive information or installing malicious software.

In this brief training, you'll learn:

  • How to identify suspicious emails
  • Common phishing tactics to watch out for
  • What to do if you suspect a phishing attempt
  • Best practices for protecting your accounts

Red Flags in Phishing Emails

Here are key warning signs to look for in suspicious emails:

  • Urgent language - Messages that create pressure to act quickly
  • Generic greetings - "Dear User" instead of your name
  • Suspicious sender email - Addresses that mimic legitimate companies but with slight variations
  • Grammar and spelling errors - Professional organisations typically proofread communications
  • Suspicious links - URLs that don't match the claimed organisation
  • Unexpected attachments - Especially executable files (.exe, .zip, etc.)
  • Requests for sensitive information - Legitimate organisations rarely ask for passwords via email

Best Practices for Email Security

Follow these guidelines to protect yourself from phishing attempts:

  • Verify the sender - Check the full email address, not just the display name
  • Hover before clicking - Place your cursor over links to see the actual URL destination
  • Be sceptical of urgency - Take time to verify requests that demand immediate action
  • Check for personalisation - Legitimate communications usually include your name
  • Use multi-factor authentication - This provides an extra layer of protection
  • Update your software - Keep your systems and security software up-to-date
  • When in doubt, reach out - Verify suspicious requests through official channels

What to Do If You Suspect a Phishing Attempt

If you receive a suspicious email, follow these steps:

  1. Don't click on links or download attachments
  2. Report the email to the IT Security team at security@[your-actual-domain].com
  3. Delete the email from your inbox
  4. If you've already clicked a link or shared information:
    • Change your passwords immediately
    • Contact IT Security right away
    • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
